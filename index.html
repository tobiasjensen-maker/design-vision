Product & Design Vision

From strategy to experience.

The product strategy is clearly centred on agentic. This vision addresses what that means for product & design: how we evolve the product AOs know today while building the product of the future — and the shared foundation that makes both possible.

Why this vision

The strategy says agentic.
270,000 companies say don't break what works.

Our product strategy for 2026/27 is clearly centred on agentic AI — three propositions, a skill-based architecture, EVA as the orchestrator. But the mass-market product doesn't stop existing when the agentic product launches. This P&D vision bridges that gap.

This vision strives to strike a balance between unlocking our agentic future and maintaining the mass-market product our AOs depend on today.

The promise

Every initiative is pushing on something real

Each bet is finding genuine customer value in its own slice — Fin.ai is resolving support tickets, Financial Insights is opening up advisory, Autosuggest is quietly removing friction in bookkeeping, Anomaly Detection is catching issues before they escalate, Praksis is shaping how AOs run their day, and EVA is earning trust inside the product itself.

These are not false starts. They are live proof that our customers want AI in their work — and that we have the domain, data, and distribution to deliver it. The last thing we should do is slow any of them down.

The reality

The customer sees six AIs, not one

Different doors. Different voices. Different onboarding. Different trust models. An accountant who meets Fin.ai in support, Financial Insights in advisory, and EVA in-product doesn't experience one intelligent partner — they experience five tools that happen to share a logo.

That fragmentation has a cost: trust is rebuilt from zero every time, value compounds nowhere, and the story we tell the market becomes a list instead of a vision. The ambition is clear; the coherence is not.

How the mass-market teams operate. Not excuses for being slow — the operating system that lets us take the right kind of risks in a regulated, trust-heavy product.

Slow is smooth, smooth is fastWe optimise for not breaking what works. The cost of a regression is measured in AO trust, support load, and churn.
Risk-managed experimentationEvery meaningful change ships behind rollout mechanics: staged exposure, AO-segment canaries, monitored success metrics, kill criteria.
Conservative prioritisationWe say no more than we say yes. A long backlog is a feature of a mature product, not a bug.
Hierarchy serves qualityDesign, engineering, compliance, and support have explicit sign-offs on customer-impacting changes.
Regulatory-grade rigorAuditability, GDPR, local tax and bookkeeping rules are non-negotiable inputs. Compliance is part of the definition of done.
Research grounded in the AOWe weight signal from the long tail of AOs — small practices, sole practitioners, apprentices — not just the loudest or most sophisticated.

How we measure whether we're succeeding — across trust, adoption, efficiency, quality, and inclusivity.

  • Trust indicators: NPS among partners and bookkeepers, retention, support contact rate, CSAT on AI-assisted interactions
  • Adoption depth: % of JTBD steps where EVA suggestions are accepted; feature-level engagement per AO
  • Efficiency gained: time-per-client per JTBD (bank reconciled, supplier invoice booked, period closed)
  • Quality: correction rate on EVA suggestions, severity-weighted incident rate
  • Inclusivity: accessibility audit scores; adoption parity across AO sizes and seniority levels

A solid, evolving design system that lets designers and developers maintain the mass-market product at a sustainable pace, without compromising quality or retention.

  • Mature existing components into fully functional ones (e.g. Table3)
  • Deep documentation for developers and designers
  • Explicit permission for teams to build "snowflakes" where the core system doesn't yet cover their need
  • Steady investment in accessibility, tokens, and consistency

Timeline: Continuous maintenance — this track compounds, it never ends.

Our agentic product with EVA as the Front Door

Stage 3 · AI is the platform

The design principles governing how the Front Door, Skills, Artefacts, and Trust Layer show up to the accounting office.

Intent over interfaceThe primary input is intent — spoken, typed, uploaded, or implied. The UI is generated in service of that intent.
AO-scale thinking by defaultEVA thinks across the entire client portfolio. "Reconcile all clients." "Close every VAT-ready client." Firm scale is the default scope.
Radical transparency of reasoningEvery action is traceable to the data, the rule, or the precedent that produced it. "Why did you do this?" is always one click away.
Agency with accountabilityAccountability lives with a named principal — the bookkeeper, the partner, the AO. Permissions and limits are first-class concepts.
Composable agents, one EVASpecialist agents cooperate behind the scenes. To the AO, it is always EVA. One voice, one memory, one identity.

The agentic track needs a different operating system — one designed for learning velocity, not just shipping velocity.

Speed is a featureWe ship daily, measure weekly, and decide monthly. If a competitor is faster, that's a strategic failure, not a neutral fact.
Small teams, short feedback loopsFull-stack squads with design, engineering, ML, and domain expertise in the same room. No handoffs. No "phase 2."
Minimal hierarchy, maximal contextDecisions happen at the level with the most context, not the highest title. Leaders unblock; they do not gatekeep.
Experience before engineeringEvery skill is an experience promise before it is a technical one. We define the UX artefact before engineering builds.
Ship to learnEvery release is an experiment. We are as proud of things we stop doing as of things we continue. Learning velocity is the primary KPI.
Reversible decisions fastMost decisions in this track are reversible — treat them that way. Irreversible decisions get careful treatment.
Design in the open — with real AOsPrototypes over specs. Live demos over decks. A rotating cohort of partner AOs is in the build loop from week one.
Higher risk tolerance — on purposeBugs are acceptable in exchange for learning. Trust-breaking incidents are not. The line between the two is a team-level craft.

How we measure learning, agent performance, trust under autonomy, and AO outcomes.

  • Learning velocity: # of experiments, % producing directional learning
  • Agent performance: task success rate, autonomous completion rate, intervention rate — per JTBD
  • Trust under autonomy: correction rate vs. confidence level, calibration accuracy, incident-free hours of autonomous operation
  • AO outcomes: clients-per-bookkeeper, revenue-per-employee, partner NPS among opt-in AOs
  • Expansion signal: % of active AOs choosing EVA-as-Front-Door over legacy surfaces when both are available

What got us here won't get us there — a new foundation for the AI-native product

A new design system capability that lets teams prototype, iterate, and deliver agentic experiences at a fundamentally different speed — so our teams can out-learn the market, not just out-ship it.

  • A new package with rewritten tokens, components, and skills/md-files purpose-built for agentic UI
  • Pattern libraries for AI interface design — trust signals, review flows, orchestration surfaces, skill templates, artefact presentation
  • A move from "Design for People" to "Design for People and AI/LLMs": components that LLMs can reason about and assemble
  • A design-system-as-product mindset, with its own roadmap, consumers, and feedback loops

Timeline: Kick off the agentic design system work by the beginning of 2027, with early patterns in the hands of agentic squads from day one.

Open questions & risks

What we don't know yet

Ambition requires honesty about the gaps. These are the questions we need to resolve as the vision matures — and the risks we manage against.

Key risks

Trust contagion

An agentic-track failure damages the mass-market brand. Mitigation: separate release surfaces, clear incident playbooks.

Two-culture split

Different ways of working fracture the org. Mitigation: shared EVA platform, deliberate rotation, unified craft.

Under-investing the core

Agentic work attracts attention; core pays the bills. Mitigation: ring-fence investment.

Design-system debt

If mass-market DS stagnates, we pay in quality and speed. Mitigation: fund both tracks of DS explicitly.

  • What's the minimum verification surface that makes autonomy feel safe?
  • Who owns the design-system-as-product function?

Two experiences. One product org.

The vision demands two tracks with different principles, pace, and design needs. But one org has to deliver both. The question isn't whether we reorganise — it's how we reshape our roles so the same people can operate across both experiences.

Organisation & roles

The line between PM and UX is dissolving

AI changes what product work looks like. When a designer can prototype with an LLM and a PM can validate with a live mockup, the traditional handoff model becomes a bottleneck. Maintaining the old boundaries will create more confusion and disruption than removing them.

The old model

Clear lanes, clean handoffs

PM
Prioritises · Writes specs · Owns backlog
UX
Researches · Designs · Hands off to dev

Works when the problem is well-scoped, the tools are stable, and discovery is a phase — not a constant. But in an AI-native product, the problem changes shape while you're solving it.

The emerging reality

Overlapping skills, shared ownership

PM
UX
Builder

Discovery, prototyping, and delivery blur together. The person closest to the problem should be able to move it forward — regardless of whether their title says PM or UX.

Why maintaining the split hurts
🐌
Handoffs slow everything down

In an AI product, the loop from insight to prototype to validation needs to be hours, not weeks. A PM-to-UX-to-dev relay can't keep up.

🧩
Context is lost at every boundary

The person who talked to the AO should be the one sketching the solution. Every translation layer loses signal.

🤖
AI compresses the skill gap

LLMs let UXers prototype with real data and PMs generate design artefacts. The tools no longer respect our org chart.

⚡
Two tracks need versatile people

The agentic track moves fast; the core track demands depth. Both need people who can flex across discovery, design, and delivery.

The moveDon't reorganise for the sake of it. Redefine the roles so the lines match where the work actually happens.
